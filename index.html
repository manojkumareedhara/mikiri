The problem

Underwater vehicles exist. The confidence to rely on them does not.

Underwater vehicles can dive deeper, operate for longer, and carry better sensors than ever. Yet most still require close human supervision. The challenge is not only vehicle capability; it is whether a vehicle can make safe, verifiable decisions when sensors and communications degrade.

71%

of Earth's surface is ocean

GPS signals do not reach underwater, communications are slow and unreliable, and live video cannot normally be streamed. A vehicle must make safe decisions on its own.

1 : 1

one pilot for each vehicle, today

Close supervision can require people and vessels at significant cost. That model limits how often subsea infrastructure can be inspected.

↑

the need for inspection is growing

Offshore wind, subsea cables, and other critical assets are expanding. The gap between what needs inspection and the people available to do it is widening.

How Mikiri works

Reliable perception. Auditable decisions.

Underwater conditions change without warning. Silt is stirred up, light fades, and sensors become unreliable. Mikiri is building a perception layer that preserves uncertainty and a decision layer that plans around it. A rule-based safety supervisor can veto unsafe actions, while defined cases are passed to a human for authorisation. Every observation and decision is logged so a mission can be understood and replayed afterwards.

What we build

Software that works across compatible underwater vehicles.

Mikiri is building software that integrates with third-party underwater vehicles. Its perception layer is designed to produce observations with uncertainty and provenance; its decision, safety, and audit layers will plan inspections, enforce safety rules, and record why each action was taken.

01

Uncertainty is made explicit.

The system is designed to track sensor quality and preserve uncertainty with each observation, responding conservatively as confidence declines.

02

Decisions are designed to be inspectable.

The system is designed to record the observations, plan, safety rules, and actions behind each decision. Operators will be able to inspect and replay what happened after a mission.

03

People remain in authority.

People will supervise the mission rather than pilot continuously. The system is designed to ask for authorisation at defined moments, while the operator can pause, abort, or surface the vehicle at any time.

04

Software that integrates across vehicles.

Vehicle-specific details are isolated so the core Mikiri software can integrate with compatible third-party vehicles rather than competing on hulls, thrusters, or sensor hardware.

Offshore wind foundations

The first mission is close inspection of monopile welds, anodes, and cable entries under human supervision.

Subsea cables and pipelines

Later applications may include cable and pipeline inspection, using the same core software with mission-specific rules.

Critical subsea infrastructure

Reliable inspection helps operators protect the infrastructure that supports energy, communications, and security.

First mission: offshore wind

Close inspection without a crewed support vessel for every vehicle.

The initial focus is supervised autonomous close inspection of offshore wind foundations: monopile welds, anodes, and cable entries. The aim is to make one vehicle more efficient under human supervision. The same architecture is designed to support multiple vehicles later, without requiring a person to pilot each one continuously.
